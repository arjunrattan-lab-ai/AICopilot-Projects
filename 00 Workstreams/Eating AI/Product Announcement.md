Eating AI Detection - Available from 2nd April 2026

What’s New?
We’re excited to announce the upcoming launch of AI-powered Eating Detection, Motive’s latest safety feature that identifies when a driver is holding or eating food while operating a commercial vehicle.

Note: Drinking is not classified as eating. AI Eating detects only solid food consumption.

How Will It Benefit Customers?

Reduce distracted driving risk: Eating and handling food can significantly increase crash risk by taking hands off the wheel and eyes off the road.
Enforce company safety & eating policies: Provides objective evidence to support policies that restrict eating while driving, especially in high-risk or regulated operations.
Enable eating related coaching & behavior change: Surfaces video-backed events in the Safety Dashboard and apps so managers can coach drivers on specific incidents.


How Does It Work?
For vehicles equipped with an AI Dashcam driver-facing camera (AIDC / AIDC+), the system will create an Eating event when:

A clearly visible edible item (food) is detected in the driver’s hand or mouth
The vehicle is traveling above 25 mph
The eating behavior is visible for 5 seconds or more


Notable Feature Limitations 
At launch, please be aware of the following:

No Drinking Detection
No self-serve threshold tuning in Dashboard (yet)
In-cab alerts available only on AIDC and not on AIDC+


Requirements 
Eating AI Detection will be available to customers who use Motive AI Dashcams (AIDC and AIDC+ both) with a driver-facing camera

Rollout Plan 
Eating AI Detection will follow this release schedule:

March 31, 2026 - SMB
April 1 - MM
April 2 - ENT/ALL


At launch:

Events will be ON by default for eligible devices.
In-cab alerts:
OFF by default
Available for AIDC and can be enabled in Safety Behavior Settings
Not initially available on AIDC+ until further model improvements.
