## The matched pair

Both research docs started from the same move: the atlas catalogs reality from Motive's lens; what's missing is the experience layer from the user's lens. Cross-reading them surfaces four themes where driver-side and FM-side are the **same problem wearing two faces**.

---

## Four cross-cutting themes

| Theme | Driver-side face | FM-side face | Joint product move |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Alert fatigue** | "Snitching" perception, tape on lens, workarounds, trust erosion | Triage burden, score volatility, dismissal read as program-failure admission | Alert prioritization by **risk** (speed × consequence × time-to-react), not by count. Fewer alerts, higher hit rate — both sides win. |
| **3am rural fatigue** | Dominant fatality mechanism. Driver is in the danger zone. DFI/drowsiness still beta. | No leading-indicator surface. FM sees it only after the crash. No Safety Score impact = no economic feedback loop. | Graduate DFI from beta (driver-side consequence) *and* expose fatigue-risk projection to FM (surface it before the event, not after). |
| **Right-turn buttonhook vs. pedestrian** | Physics-driven lane deviation (FP for driver). Blind-spot pedestrian (catastrophic for cyclist). | Coaching-noise source *and* nuclear-verdict exposure simultaneously. | Scenario-aware logic: right-turn context suppresses lane-swerve while *promoting* pedestrian warning. One architectural change resolves an FP category and opens a critical urban-fatality use case. |
| **Narrative and voice gap** | Driver can't dispute events; one-sided story in coaching (Driver Persona gap #6). | FM can't produce defensible narrative for CFO/insurer; compiles it from 4–5 surfaces. | Bidirectional narrative surface — driver context/dispute on events, FM auto-summary for exec/insurance. Same missing surface, two constituencies. |

---

## Two implications for sequencing

1. **Driver-side fixes *are* FM-side product moves.** The FM Work Research's open gaps (alert fatigue, score volatility, thin narrative) trace upstream to driver experience. Investing in driver voice, positive driving, and alert prioritization is not "driver features vs. FM features" — both sides are served by the same bets. Budget accordingly.

2. **The ADAS framing ladders directly to the exec lever map.** The Trucker Research's "dashcam AI is the ADAS of commercial trucks" is what the CFO, GC, COO, and board actually want to hear. The FM Research's upward lever map tells us how to translate that framing into each exec's language. **One story, five audiences.**
