Lane Swerving Detection Explained

How dashcam software decides if a car swerved or just changed lanes

1. The Basic Concept

The dashcam tracks a number called "position" that changes over time as the car moves sideways.

Position Scale

What position means:
• 0 = Right lane line (hugging the right edge)
• 0.5 = Center (middle of lane)
• 1 = Left lane line (hugging the left edge)

This number changes constantly as the car moves. The software looks at the shape of how it changes to decide what happened.

2. Four Different Patterns

Each pattern tells a different story about what the car did. Here's what each shape means:

Swerve ✅

A hill shape. Car drifted sideways and came back.

Should flag: Yes

Drift ❌

A ramp shape. Car gradually moved to one side.

Should flag: No

Lane Change ❌

A step shape. Car switched lanes suddenly.

Should flag: No

Boomerang ⚠️

A wide hill. Massive swerve (not a normal lane change).

Should flag: Maybe

What Each Pattern Looks Like

Swerve (Hill shape)

Car drifted right, then came back

Drift (Ramp shape)

Car gradually drifted right (never came back)

Lane Change (Step shape)

Car suddenly moved left (then stayed there)

Boomerang (Wide hill)

Massive swerve out and back (big amplitude)

3. How Swerve Detection Works

The software follows these three simple steps:

1
Find where the car stops moving sideways (Start point)

Look for a "pause" — a moment where the position stays steady for a moment before it starts moving.

2
Find the peak — where it moved the most (Peak point)

Find the furthest point — either the highest or lowest value the position reaches.

3
Find where it stops again (End point)

Look for another "pause" — another moment where the position settles down after moving.

The Question: Did we find a real hill or valley shape (movement away, then back)? Or is it something else?

If it's a real hill/valley → FLAG IT as a swerve
If it's not → DON'T flag it (it's a drift or lane change)

4. The Bug (V1) and the Fix (V2)

The original software had a problem. Here's what went wrong and how it was fixed:

V1 (Broken) ❌

The Problem:

V1 finds three pause points on almost any sideways movement, even ramps. So a gentle, continuous drift gets flagged as a swerve.

Result: False positives (flags drifts as swerves)

V2 (Fixed) ✅

The Solution:

V2 checks something extra: did the position drop by at least 0.15 on each side of the peak, with at least one side dropping 0.30 or more? Ramps fail this test.

Result: Only real swerves get flagged

Side-by-Side Example: How They Differ

Scenario: A car drifts slowly to the right (a ramp)

V1's Mistake:

Finds 3 points, says "SWERVE!"
Wrong ❌

V2's Fix:

Checks drop size, says "Not a swerve"
Right ✅

5. Quick Reference: All Four Patterns

Pattern Shape Should Be Flagged? V1 Gets It Right? V2 Gets It Right?
Swerve Hill or valley YES ✅ Yes Yes
Drift Ramp (one-way slide) NO ❌ No (flags it) Yes
Lane Change Step (sudden jump) NO ❌ Yes Yes
Boomerang Wide hill (big amplitude) MAYBE ⚠️ Yes (flags it) Sometimes
The Key Insight:
A swerve is when a car moves sideways and comes back. Just moving sideways (drift) or changing lanes (step) isn't a swerve. V1 couldn't tell the difference, but V2 can.
