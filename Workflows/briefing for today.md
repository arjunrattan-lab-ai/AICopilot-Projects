Three practical systems for your situation
1. Use the workspace you've already built — but add a weekly log

You have workstream folders (EVE, AIDC+, UK Expansion, etc.) with Context.md files and Running Tasks. What's missing is a weekly synthesis — a 15-min Friday ritual where you write 3-5 bullets per workstream: what changed, what's blocked, what I need to decide next week.

This isn't for anyone else. It's for Monday-you to remember what Friday-you knew.

2. Separate "learn" from "decide" from "delegate"

Right now everything feels equally urgent because it's all new. Try bucketing:

Bucket	What goes here	Your action
Learn	Pipeline mechanics, data model, model precision details	Read docs, ask questions, trace events — you're doing this well
Decide	EVE trial rollout scope, distraction definition change, Q2 priorities	These have deadlines. Flag them. Don't let learning block deciding.
Delegate	Threshold tuning, Statsig config changes, dashboard creation	Know enough to review, not enough to do. Trust the engineers.
The overwhelm comes from treating "learn" items with "decide" urgency. Most of what you're learning this week is context that pays off in month 2-3, not next Monday.

3. Pick 2-3 things to be known for in Q2

You can't own everything. From what I've seen, the highest-leverage bets for you are:

EVE/CBB rollout — You already have the deepest context. Shoaib asked for a timeline. Nihar tagged you to own planning. This is your #1 deliverable.

Everything else (VLM video search, driver personas, data analysis queries) is supporting material, not your scoreboard.

What to stop doing
Stop trying to learn every system equally deep. You went 5 tables deep in Snowflake in one session — that's great for building intuition, but you don't need to be the person who runs SQL queries as a director.
Stop worrying about forgetting. You have Context.md files, meeting summaries, and a searchable workspace. The point of this system is that you don't have to remember — you have to know where to look.
