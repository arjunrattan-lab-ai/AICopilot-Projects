## Backing / Reversing

**Industry fatality rate:**
Refuse and recyclable material collectors recorded a fatality rate of **41.4 per 100,000 full-time equivalent workers in 2023** — the 4th deadliest occupation in the US. Of 35 fatalities in solid waste collection that year, 24 (69%) were transportation incidents.
- [Waste Dive — BLS CFOI 2023 coverage](https://www.wastedive.com/news/waste-recycling-worker-fatality-rate-2024/735975/)
- [BLS CFOI Summary 2024](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/cfoi.nr0.htm)
- [Waste Today on 2023 rate increase](https://www.wastetodaymagazine.com/news/fatality-rate-increased-for-waste-collection-in-2023-bureau-of-labor-data-says/)

**Backing-specific rate (historical):**
NIOSH tracked 450 refuse worker fatalities from 1980–1992. Of 110 vehicle-strike deaths, **20 (18%) occurred specifically when the truck was backing.** Most recent federal figure that isolates backing as a cause category. — [NIOSH Alert 97-110](https://www.cdc.gov/niosh/docs/97-110/default.html)

**NHTSA backover data (all vehicles):**
Backing crashes cause approximately **250+ fatalities and 18,000+ injuries annually** across all vehicle types. Pedestrians account for ~96% of backover fatalities. No NHTSA dataset isolates commercial fleet backing fatalities specifically.
- [NHTSA Estimation of Backover Fatalities](https://www.nhtsa.gov/document/estimation-backover-fatalities)
- [NHTSA Backover Avoidance Technology Study](https://one.nhtsa.gov/Vehicle-Safety/Vehicle-Backover-Avoidance-Technology-Study-%E2%80%93-Report-to-Congress)

**OSHA regulatory basis:**
29 CFR 1926.601(b)(4) requires vehicles with obstructed rear view to use a backup alarm or spotter when reversing. OSHA data: over 70 workers killed from backover incidents in a single year (2011 BLS).
- [OSHA Preventing Backovers — Standards](https://www.osha.gov/preventing-backovers/standards)
- [OSHA Preventing Backovers — Overview](https://www.osha.gov/preventing-backovers)

## Right-Turn Blind Spot

**Transit fatality data:**
From 2008–2023, transit buses reported **8,230 bus-to-person collisions** resulting in **596 fatalities** (14% of all transit fatalities) and 8,259 injuries. FTA issued Safety Advisory 23-1 (September 2023) recommending pedestrian turn warning systems.
- [FTA Safety Advisory 23-1](https://www.transit.dot.gov/sites/fta.dot.gov/files/2023-09/FTA-Safety-Advisory-23-1-Bus-to-Person-Collisions.pdf)

**NYC data:**
In 2023, trucks (excluding pickups) accounted for 12% of NYC pedestrian fatalities and buses for 9% — disproportionate to vehicle miles traveled. MTA has equipped 285 buses with Pedestrian Turn Warning Systems, naming turns as the primary high-risk event type.
- [NYC DOT Vision Zero 2022 fatalities release](https://www.nyc.gov/html/dot/html/pr2023/vision-zero-fatalities-dropped-2022.shtml)
- [NYC Vision Zero View interactive data](https://vzv.nyc/)

**Regulatory basis:**
- FTA Safety Advisory 23-1 (Sept 2023): recommends transit agencies deploy pedestrian turn warning systems — the closest thing to a federal mandate. [FTA link](https://www.transit.dot.gov/regulations-and-programs/safety/bus-person-collisions-safety-advisory-23-1)
- NYC DOT Vision Zero + MTA 285-bus turn-warning deployment — named risk is large vehicle turning.
- UK DVS mandate: requires MOIS + BSIS on HGVs in London — the hardest global regulatory precedent. [DVS overview](https://fleetdvs.co.uk/direct-vision-standard/)

## Other Claim Patterns (from AIOC-V1-Use-Cases.md UC11–32)

**Rare but catastrophic:**
- UC18: Reverse hit-and-run, pedestrian behind rolling truck — "the waste-worker death scenario." No current driver defense. Rare, highest consequence.
- UC32: Child runs up to stopped waste truck during residential pickup. 500–1,000 stops per route per day = massive daily exposure. Driver's only defense: mirror sweep and honk.
- UC12: Pedestrian struck during turn — forward dashcam misses the event entirely. "Rare per fleet, but high-dollar."

**Fraud and disputed-liability cluster (moderate dollar, rising frequency):**
- UC26: Clean Harbors pattern — pedestrian walks into stopped truck, falls, calls ambulance. No side/rear footage to exonerate. "Trending up in some urban markets."
- UC15: Pedestrian walks into side of moving truck — shared-fault or staged fraud. "Few per year per large urban fleet."
- UC13: Sidewalk mirror-contact claim — "several per year per large urban fleet," driver's word vs. pedestrian's.
- UC27: Slip-and-fall near parked vehicle — premises-liability, "nothing today" to defend.

**OSHA and worker comp cluster:**
- UC29: Worker injury near stopped truck — OSHA citation + shutdown + worker comp. "Multiple per large fleet per year."
- UC30: Delivery/mail truck pedestrian injury claim. "Rare per route, multiple per year per large fleet."
- UC17: Disputed yard low-speed incident — "multiple per large fleet per year."

**Backing exposure anchor:** Waste residential routes generate dozens of backing events per vehicle per day (route-density dependent; not independently verified). Each is a potential UC11 (claim without evidence) or UC18 (fatality) with zero current video defense.

## Data Gaps

1. **Named backing-specific waste verdicts at $10M+** — not found publicly. Most settle under NDA. The $27M Fried Goldberg backing case and $38.8M Republic right-turn case are the strongest public anchors.
2. **NHTSA commercial fleet maneuver breakdown** — FARS does not publish a clean table of pedestrian fatalities by maneuver type for commercial vehicles specifically.
3. **NWRA published statement** — no recent standalone data report found. The NIOSH 1997 Alert is the cleanest federal source isolating backing as a cause category.
4. **NYC right-turn fatality count by vehicle type** — NYC Vision Zone View has the raw data but no published fact sheet isolating "right-turn fatalities by heavy vehicle type."
