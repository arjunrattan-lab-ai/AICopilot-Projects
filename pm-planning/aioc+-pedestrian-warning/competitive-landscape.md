## Samsara AI Multicam

**What they sell:** A hardware hub (HW-MC-AIM4) that ingests up to four auxiliary 1080p camera feeds and runs on-device AI detection across all streams, delivering real-time in-cab alerts and 360° live visibility. The named AI detection for VRU is **Pedestrian Collision Warning** — covers forward (dash cam), side, and rear simultaneously. ([AI Multicam product page](https://www.samsara.com/products/models/ai-multicam))

**SKU / bundling:** Separate add-on hardware SKU, not bundled into base platform. Requires existing Samsara subscription. Hardware list price: **$2,160** per hub. ([LTT Partners reseller listing](https://www.lttpartners.com/products/samsara-ai-multicam)) Software add-on pricing is quote-only. All-in platform: ~$40–52/vehicle/month (3-year contract). ([AirPinpoint pricing analysis](https://airpinpoint.com/compare/samsara-pricing))

1. Liability / exoneration first — *"Exonerate drivers with definitive video evidence from synchronized HD footage across multiple cameras."* Fatal truck crash = $7.2M average (FMCSA). ([Latest AI detections blog](https://www.samsara.com/blog/latest-ai-detections))
2. Safety ROI second — insurance premium reduction (Sprint Waste: ~$500K saved), incident rate reduction (Coach USA: 75% drop in total claim costs).
3. Compliance a distant third — DVS framing appears in UK materials only.

**Vocation anchors:** No dedicated vocation landing pages for AI Multicam. Key signals:

## Cross-competitor headline

| Competitor | Side/Rear Ped Detection | Trigger Logic |
|---|---|---|
| **Samsara AI Multicam** | ✅ Shipping | **Gated** — turn signal OR reverse only (contradicts "continuous AI" marketing) |
| **Netradyne D-810 / Hub-X** | ❌ Not shipping — "Upcoming auxiliary camera intelligence" on May 2025 spec | n/a (D-810 roadmap, 6-12 months out) |
| **Lytx / Surfsight AI-14** | ❌ Structural gap — aux cams are video-only, no on-device AI | n/a (no roadmap disclosed) |
| **Pro-Vision Birdseye** | ✅ Shipping | **Continuous** — AI runs on all cameras; "7 triggers" are view-switching, not AI gates |

**The V1 strategic question this reframes:** gated (Samsara) or continuous (Pro-Vision)? Gated matches most competitor baselines, misses stopped-yard waste (WC landfill, GreenWaste, Ace). Continuous matches waste demand but inherits Pro-Vision's FP-bar problem (Ace bollards/cones/mailboxes). The market is effectively **1-competitor (Samsara) + 1-partner-competitor (Pro-Vision)** for the V1 window — Netradyne and Lytx are 6-12+ months out.

## Samsara AI Multicam — Deep Dive

**Architecture:** Edge-inference hub (HW-MC-AIM4) running Pedestrian Collision Warning on-device across all 4 aux streams. Hub has **no speaker** — in-cab audio routes through the paired AI Dash Cam. Storage 40 hrs HD / 500+ with 1TB SD. [[samsara-1]](https://www.samsara.com/pdf/ai-multicam.pdf)

**Form factor:** 187×127×43 mm IP67 hub; up to 4× 1080p **AHD (analog HD, 4-pin GX12)** cameras; optional 7" analog in-cab monitor; 6 total streams with dash cam. [[samsara-1]](https://www.samsara.com/pdf/ai-multicam.pdf)

**Scenarios covered:**

| Scenario | Supported? | Trigger | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Backing (reverse) | ✅ | Reverse gear | Explicitly called out in Coach USA interview |
| Right-turn blind spot | ✅ | Turn signal | "AI scans drivable area when driver activates turn signal" |
| Left-turn blind spot | ✅ | Turn signal | Symmetric |
| Moving blind-spot (>5 mph, lane change) | Partial | Turn signal; no explicit speed gate | Motive SE saw cyclist detection [encounter log row 14] |
| **Stopped yard / near-field** | ❌ **Not confirmed** | **No public trigger** | **Open lane — WC landfill, GreenWaste, Ace live here** |
| Forward crosswalk (dash cam) | ✅ | Continuous on dash cam | Separate system from Multicam |
| Cyclists, scooters, road workers | ✅ | Same turn/reverse gates | Same class as ped |

**Critical contradiction:** Samsara marketing says "AI on all streams"; Coach USA article exposes that side/rear PCW is **event-triggered (turn signal OR reverse), not continuous**. [[samsara-6]](https://www.metro-magazine.com/articles/how-coach-usa-is-using-ai-to-prevent-bus-accidents)

**Alerting — driver side:** Audio routes through AI Dash Cam speaker (hub has none). **Voice prompt verbatim text not published** in any datasheet or demo. On detection the relevant camera feed is **highlighted** on the 7" monitor; bounding box visible in product-page screenshot. Live monitor shows continuous feed by default. No published latency number. No documented backoff/cooldown.

**Alerting — fleet manager side:** Events flow to **Safety Inbox** with 1-min HD clip (up to 10 min on-demand; 40 min hyperlapse). **Alert/event decoupling is real** — Capterra review and internal Weisiger signal both confirm: in-cab voice fires without the corresponding event landing in the Safety Inbox. A secondary cloud-side AI layer filters events reaching managers but does NOT filter driver alerts. Driver eats the FPs; manager is shielded.

**Pricing / packaging:** Hub ~$2,160 list (LTT Partners). Platform $40/veh/mo in competitive bids (Joe Stead PubSec), often with free hardware + waived install. 3-year standard contract. Required base: Samsara subscription + AI Dash Cam.

**Customer reactions:**
- *"Drivers sometimes get an in-cab voice alert but the event/video never shows up in the safety inbox."* — Safety Manager, Capterra
- *"The real-time in-cab features were a distraction to their drivers — actually a negative."* — Travis Young (Motive AE) on Weisiger trial loss
- *"Samsara relied on a secondary AI layer to check for false positives… in-cab alerts were still triggered — the system only filtered what was sent into the backend, not what the drivers experienced."* — Jessica Demarest (Motive AE), Norfolk Southern dinner notes

**Top 2 insights for V1:**
1. **Gated trigger leaves stopped-yard open.** Samsara has no stopped-in-gear trigger — where WC landfill, GreenWaste, and Ace actually live.
2. **Commit to alert-event coupling as V1 launch requirement** — if Motive fires in-cab, it's reviewable in Safety Inbox. Visible positioning contrast.

## Pro-Vision (Ranger + Birdseye AI DVR) — Deep Dive

**Architecture:**
- **Ranger DVR (PD-1902):** 10-channel hybrid HD DVR (8 AHD + 1 DHD), zero-latency passthrough, up to 1 TB SD. **No on-device AI.** CloudConnect VMS optional.
- **Birdseye AI DVR:** Dedicated AI control box (6.5″×5.8″×1.5″, IP68), up to 6 AHD in, 30 FPS. **On-device AI detections: pedestrians, vehicles, forward-collision hazards.** Image stitching in real time produces 2D/3D top-down view. Compute platform not publicly disclosed (not Orin-class).
- **Side View Radar Kit** = separate add-on today, not fused with vision. Low-speed mode (<10 mph), 11.5 ft reach. Post-Spartan acquisition, fusion is **roadmap, not ship**.

**Form factor:**

| Component | Ranger | Birdseye |
|---|---|---|
| DVR / control box | PD-1902, 10-ch, 1TB SD | 6.5×5.8×1.5 in, IP68, 6 AHD in |
| Cameras | 1080p AHD, IP69K | 4 or 6 ultra-wide AHD, 200° FOV option |
| In-cab display | Separate monitor | **7" HD touchscreen, 19 display configs** |
| Triggers | Reverse, turn, CAN, custom | **7 view-switch triggers** (turn ×2, reverse, CAN ×2, custom ×2) |

**Scenarios covered:**

| Scenario | Ranger | Birdseye | Trigger | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Backing | Video + live monitor only | ✅ AI + reverse-triggered view | Reverse | Auto-displays Birdseye view |
| Right-turn blind spot | Live monitor switch (no AI) | ✅ AI + blinker view switch | Turn signal | |
| Left-turn blind spot | Same | ✅ | Turn signal | |
| Moving blind-spot (>5 mph) | No AI | **Continuous AI** — vision-only; Side Radar Kit is <10 mph only | AI detection (not vehicle-signal-gated) | Weaker at high speed |
| **Stopped yard / near-field (Ace case)** | No AI | ✅ **Continuous AI on all cameras** | AI detection | **This is where Ace's FPs on bollards/cones/mailboxes come from** |
| Forward crosswalk | Video only | Listed as "forward-collision hazards" | | Overlaps AIDC+ FPW domain |

**Critical trigger-logic correction:** Birdseye's "7 triggers" are for **view-switching on the monitor**, NOT for gating AI. The AI detections run **continuously** on all cameras and can themselves trigger view switches. Internal framing of Birdseye as "blinker-triggered like Samsara" is wrong.

**Alerting — driver side:** 7" HD touchscreen auto-switches to Birdseye + relevant side camera when trigger fires. AI-powered audible + visual alerts for ped/vehicle/FCW. Bounding box overlay implied (product page shows it on Side Radar Kit). **Radar is a separate parallel alert layer today, not fused with vision** — AE pitch ("fusion makes it way more accurate") is running ahead of the ship spec.

**Alerting — fleet manager side (the load-bearing gap):**
- Pro-Vision CloudConnect = Pro-Vision's own web VMS; receives Birdseye footage + AI event metadata
- Motive Help Center describes Pro-Vision AI DVR events producing alerts *"on the in-cab monitor"* only — **NOT** in Motive Fleet Dashboard [[provision-10]](https://helpcenter.gomotive.com/hc/en-us/articles/34889025470237)
- Glean synthesis (2026-04-22): *"ped detection events do NOT flow into the Motive dashboard or platform. The detection and alerts remain local to Pro-Vision hardware and in-cab monitor."*
- Side/rear video clips only attach to **Motive-originated** events (driver capture, collision, UNSAFE_LANE_CHANGE, FCW). Birdseye-originated ped events don't land on a Motive safety event card.
- **Net:** customer running Motive + Pro-Vision sees ped alerts in cab but loses that event from the FM's coaching workflow. Two panes of glass.

**Pricing / packaging:**

| Product | Hardware | Subscription |
|---|---|---|
| Ranger DVR | Quote only (~$600-1,200 class) | CloudConnect optional |
| Birdseye AI | **~$1,200 adder** on Motive co-sell (internal) | Not publicly disclosed; routes through CloudConnect not Motive stack |
| Side Radar Kit | Quote only | Local hardware alerts only |

Billing-relationship fork: Motive + Pro-Vision AI subscription splits across two vendors.

**Customer reactions:**
- **Ace Disposal:** Birdseye generates FPs on *"bollards, mailboxes, cones"* — SLC residential side-loader, Truck 4067. Detection zone deliberately left wide post-install. **This is Birdseye's friendliest use case** (low-speed, high-object-density). If FP-heavy here, it's not a differentiated vision stack.
- **GreenWaste Recovery:** In trial, asked specifically for Birdseye's ped detection. Demand real, product performance TBD.
- **FirstFleet / Werner:** Tractor/trailer — **Birdseye doesn't fit**. DAK flagged *"360 AI doesn't work with tractor/trailers."*
- **Primo Brands:** MSA commits to AI ped detection; buying Ranger + Gen2/3 cameras (not the AI product). **Expectation risk** — betting on Pro-Vision's AI roadmap.

**Top 2 insights for V1:**
1. **Integration gap is an architectural billing-and-workflow fork, not just a data gap.** V1 has two paths: (a) native AIOC+ ped on Motive Omnicam (unified pane, own precision+billing); or (b) event-ingestion layer for Birdseye output (faster ship, cedes precision bar and billing permanently). Director sequencing call.
2. **Birdseye is AI-continuous, not blinker-gated.** Ace's stationary-yard FPs only exist because Birdseye runs continuously. If V1 scopes to blinker-gated only, we ship narrower than our partner-competitor and cede the stopped-yard quadrant that drives waste-sector demand.

## 3rd Eye — Addendum (context only, not a direct competitor)

GreenWaste Recovery is actively displacing 3rd Eye (contract up May 2025; *"Right side driver alerts are a must to switch from 3rd Eye"* — Scott Caput, Oct 2024). 3rd Eye is a **legacy video-evidence DVR** — multi-camera recording + in-cab live view, **no on-device AI detection**. Comparable architecturally to Pro-Vision Ranger, not Birdseye. Positioned as the incumbent that loses when any AI-enabled alternative enters the evaluation. Displacement path for AIOC+ V1 is the same motion we'd run against any non-AI DVR: "add detection to what you already record." No deeper competitive research warranted.
