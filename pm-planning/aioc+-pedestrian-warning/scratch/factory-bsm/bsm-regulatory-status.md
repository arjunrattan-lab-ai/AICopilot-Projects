# BSM Regulatory Status — Heavy Trucks

_Research date: 2026-04-22_

## TL;DR

**No active US federal mandate for heavy-duty BSM, and none is likely to land inside a 2–3 year window.** The EU already mandates BSIS on new trucks (in force since July 2024), and the UK's DVS Progressive Safe System (PSS) effectively requires BSIS + MOIS on HGVs operating in Greater London since Oct 2024. Both EU/UK regs apply to **new vehicle type approvals and London-permit operation** — they do not retrofit the existing US fleet Motive sells into. In the US, NHTSA's pending activity is on **side underride guards** and light-vehicle BSW (NCAP), not heavy-truck BSM. Factory-standard BSM on US Class 8 in the next 2–3 years is unlikely via regulation; OEM voluntary adoption is the more plausible path.

## US — NHTSA / FMCSA / FMVSS

- **No FMVSS mandate for heavy-truck BSM.** No rulemaking in progress that would require BSIS/BSM on Class 7–8 trucks.
- **FMVSS 127 (AEB)** — mandates AEB + pedestrian AEB on **passenger cars and light trucks** (≤10,000 lb GVWR) by Sept 2029. Does not cover heavy trucks.
- **FMVSS 111 (Rearview Mirrors)** — prior NPRM considered rear object detection on straight trucks 10k–26k lb GVWR. No final rule for heavy-duty BSM.
- **Side underride** — 2023 NHTSA ANPRM on side underride guards for trailers; preliminary analysis showed ~$1.2B/yr cost for <20 lives/yr saved. FMCSA has **denied** a correction request on its side-underride review — effectively cold.
- **FMCSA** posture on blind spots is **educational** (Share the Road, "No Zone" campaigns), not regulatory.
- **BSW NCAP test procedure (Nov 2024)** — light-vehicle only, up to 10,000 lb GVWR. Not applicable to our segment.

## US — state-level

- **No state mandate** for BSM on heavy trucks surfaced in search. NYC's DOT has Vision Zero truck side-guard rules for **city-contracted fleets only** (procurement-driven, not a motor vehicle standard).
- School bus states occasionally mandate cameras/stop-arm enforcement — not BSM for Class 8 linehaul/vocational.

## UK — DVS (Direct Vision Standard)

- **TfL-administered**, applies to HGVs >12t operating in Greater London. Not a UK-wide motor vehicle standard.
- **Oct 28, 2024**: minimum star rating raised from 1 → 3. Sub-3-star trucks must install the **Progressive Safe System (PSS)** to get a permit.
- **PSS required kit:**
  - Camera Monitoring System (nearside) or Class V/VI mirrors
  - **Blind Spot Information System (BSIS)** — active sensors warning of VRUs
  - **Moving Off Information System (MOIS)** — front sensor for VRUs
  - Side under-run protection (both sides, unless impractical)
  - Audible left-turn warning
- Permits valid until **Oct 28, 2030**. £550 fine per non-compliant entry.
- **OEM response:** DAF, Mercedes, Scania, Volvo now ship DVS-compliant cab designs for UK/EU market. Aftermarket kits (Brigade, Motormax, AIDA) dominate retrofit on older units.

## EU — General Safety Regulation (GSR II)

- **Regulation (EU) 2019/2144**, applies to new vehicle **type approvals from July 2022** and **all new registrations from July 7, 2024**.
- **Mandatory on new trucks/buses from July 2024** (8 features including):
  - **Blind Spot Information System (BSIS)** — cyclist/pedestrian warning
  - Moving-off Information System (for trucks/buses)
  - Intelligent Speed Assistance, AEB, driver drowsiness, lane-keeping, reversing detection, event data recorder
- **2029**: enhanced **direct vision** cab requirements take effect (lower beltlines, larger glazed areas) — this is the "factory BSM + glass cab" wave.
- Estimated 25,000 fatalities prevented by 2038.

## Implications for AIOC+ V1

- **Regulation does NOT force factory BSM standard on US heavy trucks in the 2–3 year window.** AIOC+ retrofit TAM in the US is not regulation-threatened.
- **EU/UK are different.** If AIOC+ targets UK/EU operators, we are competing against (a) factory-fitted GSR-compliant tractors on new orders from July 2024 onward, and (b) a saturated DVS retrofit market (Brigade, Motormax, AIDA) with established TfL-compliant kits. V1 differentiation in those markets must be **AI quality** (fewer nuisance alerts) and **integration with Motive camera/telematics** — not the existence of a warning system.
- **US V1 window is clean.** Factory BSM on Class 8 is an **OEM voluntary adoption** question (Volvo VNL, Freightliner Cascadia, Kenworth T680 offer optional side radar today, not standard). Adoption is slow and fragmented — retrofit remains the dominant path for 3+ years.
- **Watch items:**
  - NHTSA heavy-truck AEB rulemaking (separate from FMVSS 127) — could bundle pedestrian detection.
  - CARB / California ZEV truck regs occasionally pull in ADAS requirements.
  - NYC, Boston, Seattle procurement rules for city-contracted fleets — niche but growing.
