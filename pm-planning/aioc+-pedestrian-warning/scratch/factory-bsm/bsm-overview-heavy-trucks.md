# Factory BSM — Heavy Truck Industry Overview

## Headline
Factory BSM is **available but not yet universally standard** on new Class 5-8 trucks in the US. It ships standard on select new flagship tractors (e.g., Mack Pioneer "Mack Protect Pro", Volvo VNL safety package) and is an optional spec on most other heavy-duty nameplates. In Europe, BSIS is already regulation-mandated on all new heavy vehicles (UN ECE R151 / R159, GSR 2019/2144, mandatory July 2022). Most US heavy-truck BSM volume today still flows through Tier-1 supplier channels (Bendix BlindSpotter, ZF OnSide, Bosch) spec'd at order time or retrofit — i.e., a factory-option-plus-aftermarket hybrid, not a universal factory-standard feature.

## Timeline of adoption
- **Pre-2020:** BSM on Class 7-8 was almost exclusively aftermarket or fleet-retrofit via Bendix, ZF/WABCO, Mobileye. No US Class 8 OEM shipped it as a default.
- **2022:** EU GSR / UN ECE R151 / R158 / R159 become mandatory (July 6, 2022) — every new heavy commercial vehicle sold in the EU must have a Blind Spot Information System for VRUs.
- **2023-2024:** US Class 8 OEMs introduce BSM as a factory-order spec (Volvo VNL, Freightliner Cascadia via Detroit Assurance, International LT via Bendix Wingman Fusion + BlindSpotter, Peterbilt/Kenworth via SmartLINQ/Bendix).
- **2025:** Mack launches the new Pioneer with **Mack Protect Pro** making blind-spot + lane-change support standard at that trim; Volvo VNL lists blind-spot detection as a listed safety feature on the new platform. Penske reports "more than half of carriers have implemented three or more new safety technologies," with blind-spot detection among them.
- **2026 trajectory:** Moving toward factory-default on flagship highway tractors; still optional or aftermarket on vocational, medium-duty (Class 5-6), and older nameplates. No US federal mandate equivalent to EU GSR yet.

| Peterbilt / Kenworth (Class 8) | **Optional** — Bendix-based spec | |
| Medium-duty (Class 5-7, M2, F-650/750) | **Mostly optional or aftermarket** | Limited factory BSM offerings |

Tier-1 suppliers — Bendix (BlindSpotter, Wingman Fusion), ZF (OnSide BSD, 24 GHz radar), Bosch (CV blind-spot), Mobileye Shield+, Stoneridge MirrorEye — power both the factory and retrofit channels. Bendix explicitly markets BlindSpotter as either "spec as part of your next new truck order, or upgrade your current fleet through our retrofit program," confirming the dual channel.

## Key findings
- **No US regulatory mandate** for factory BSM on Class 5-8 today. EU has one (GSR 2019/2144); US does not, which is the primary reason factory-standard adoption lags.
- **Flagship Class 8 highway tractors are the leading edge.** Mack Pioneer (2025) and new Volvo VNL are the first US Class 8 platforms to make BSM effectively standard on top safety trims. Cascadia, International, Peterbilt, Kenworth still treat it as a paid option.
- **Class 5-7 and vocational remain aftermarket-heavy.** Medium-duty and vocational segments (dump, refuse, box trucks) rely overwhelmingly on Bendix/ZF/Mobileye retrofit kits.
- **Tier-1 supplier concentration.** Even when factory-fit, the hardware is almost always Bendix, ZF, or Bosch — the same hardware available aftermarket. Factory integration provides cleaner HMI (mirror indicators, dash integration) but not necessarily better sensing.
- **Coverage gap vs pedestrian/VRU.** Most factory BSM on US tractors is tuned for **vehicle-to-vehicle** lane-change detection (adjacent lanes, passing vehicles). **VRU-focused BSIS** (pedestrians, cyclists at low speed / urban) — which is what EU R151 mandates — is still rare as a factory spec on US Class 8. This is the wedge for aftermarket / AIOC+ pedestrian warning.
- **Fleet adoption outpaces factory-standard.** ATRI via Penske: >50% of carriers have implemented three or more new safety techs; adoption driven by insurance pressure, not OEM defaults.
