# Factory BSM — Refuse Trucks

## TL;DR
**Factory BSM is optional, not standard, on every major North American refuse chassis.** Mack is the only OEM with a purpose-built OEM-integrated VRU/blind-spot radar system (Sensata PreView, launched at WasteExpo 2022 for the LR / LR Electric) — and even that is a paid option. Peterbilt 520, Autocar ACX/E-ACX, and Freightliner 114SD offer pre-wire / camera-ready harnesses and collision-mitigation integration, but the blind-spot / pedestrian detection system itself is sourced from third parties (Bendix, Mobileye, VIA, Orlaco, Preco) and either spec'd as an option or bolted on post-delivery by the body builder or fleet. **Net: the retrofit TAM is effectively the entire in-service refuse fleet, regardless of model year**, because even a 2024 build is very unlikely to have factory pedestrian-grade BSM from the OEM line.

## By OEM

- **Mack LR / LR Electric:** Sensata **PreView** 4-radar VRU detection system (front, rear, both sides) launched at **WasteExpo 2022** (MY22+). **Factory-installed option** on new builds; dealer retrofit available. A-pillar lights flash + audible alert on VRU detection. Not standard — customer must spec it.
- **Peterbilt 520:** No OEM-branded BSM/pedestrian system. Brochure highlights the 7" digital display is "fully integrated with collision mitigation systems" and RP170/joystick pre-wiring for body integration — i.e., ready for third-party BSM, not factory-fitted. Forward collision mitigation (Bendix Wingman Fusion) is available as an option; side/rear pedestrian detection is not OEM standard.
- **Autocar ACX / E-ACX:** Factory pre-wire for **chassis camera, cab camera, and overhead monitor/IFM display** (per 2021 ACX refuse sales aid). Camera system is factory-integrated at the harness level, but the actual camera/BSM package is spec'd optionally. No OEM-branded VRU radar. Autocar markets integration readiness for third-party safety kits; system itself is optional.
- **Freightliner 114SD:** Detroit Assurance suite (adaptive cruise, active brake, side object detection via Bendix BlindSpotter radar) is **optional**, primarily targeted at on-highway Cascadia; on 114SD vocational refuse configs, side-object radar is a spec'd option, not standard. No OEM pedestrian-grade detection baked in.
- **Other (McNeilus/Heil body builders):** Body builders routinely install aftermarket BSM/pedestrian kits (VIA Mobile360, Orlaco, Preco, Rosco) at upfit — again, fleet-spec'd, not chassis-OEM-standard.

## Implications for AIOC+ V1
- **Retrofit TAM = full refuse fleet, all vintages.** No OEM ships a pedestrian-grade BSM as a no-cost standard feature. Even brand-new 2024–2026 builds are dominantly shipped *without* it unless the fleet paid to spec the option.
- **Mack PreView is the only OEM-native competitor to watch** — Sensata radar, factory-wired, A-pillar HMI. For Mack LR customers who opted in from 2022+, AIOC+ would overlap. But adoption rate of the option is unknown and likely low given the upcharge and the fact that refuse fleets historically under-spec safety options.
- **The factory integration gap is the wedge.** OEMs offer pre-wire and harness readiness but leave the actual detection system to the fleet. AIOC+ slots directly into that gap — a retrofit-first, vision-based pedestrian system that doesn't depend on chassis OEM spec decisions made at order time (often 12–18 months before delivery).
- **No "factory BSM already covers us" objection holds up** across Peterbilt, Autocar, or Freightliner vocational lines. Mack LR is the one account-level objection to prepare for.
