## Part 2 — Summary (≤250 words)

- Segment pattern. Provision demand is concentrated in three segments: waste services (Athens, Ace Disposal, GreenWaste, Waste Connections/WM/GFL prospects, CapMetro), transit/school bus (NYCSBUS, Landmark, Harlow's, Buffalo), and construction/beverage distribution (Liberty Coke, Primo Brands, CRH/Preferred Materials, FirstFleet, Werner). Within those: waste + transit buy the Birdseye AI DVR for pedestrian/blind-spot alerting; construction, beverage and long-haul buy the Ranger (non-AI) DVR primarily for side/rear video evidence and in-cab monitoring to replace an existing RVS/Lytx/3rd Eye tablet. The word "AI" rarely appears in the customer ask — "in-cab monitor," "back-up assist," "blind spot when blinker is on," and "don't rip my existing cameras" do.
- What they actually asked for. By frequency of ADAS bucket: (1) Video evidence only / in-cab monitor ≈ 40%, (2) Moving-into-moving BSM lane-change/turn (blinker-triggered side view) ≈ 25%, (3) Stationary blind-spot (ped detection at curb/yard) ≈ 20%, (4) Backing rear cross-traffic ≈ 10%, (5) Multi-feature/unclear ≈ 5%. The clearest, repeated customer phrasing for AI is Ace Disposal + Athens asking for pedestrian detection on a residential side-loader in urban curb conditions.
- Integration-gap impact. The "events don't flow" issue is named across beta customers (Pariso 12/11, Mayer's Feb, FirstFleet 1/27, CRH 3/31) and has already produced one confirmed closed-lost — Paul Logistics, $275k CCV, SFDC note: "ProVision integration was a deal breaker. Needed yesterday." CRH/Preferred Materials raised model-coverage concerns mid-trial ("not seeing ANY AI models outside of distraction, smoking, and a single hard braking event"). Ace Disposal flagged pedestrian FPs from bollards/mailboxes with no cloud video to review. Provision does not take back used HW, so lost trials = Motive owns inventory.
- Competitive dynamics. Provision has been the explicit counter to Samsara AI Multicam in at least six deals (Athens, FirstFleet, Primo, Liberty Coke, Kaiser, Werner, NYCSBUS). Motive wins on native resolution, AI models on the DVR, and Birdseye view (not in Samsara). Motive loses or softens when: (a) prospect is tractor/trailer (Birdseye does not support articulating vehicles — confirmed by DAK on FirstFleet), (b) prospect is yellow-iron/forklift (Kevin Durand — four stuck deals, pointed to Proxicam), (c) PV hardware shipped without Motive SO# causing install delay, making the customer compare against Samsara's cheaper HD Camera Connector. Netradyne+PV combo has been cited as a specific risk in Liberty Coke. Internally, Ling Lee has twice stated Pro-Vision "should only be pitched if you'll lose the deal without" — i.e., Motive treats it as a stop-gap, not a strategic SKU.

## Implication for AIOC+ V1 pedestrian warning scoping

Three signals worth elevating into the scoping doc:

1. Customer-voice for AI is narrow. Of 50 encounters, fewer than 10 have customer-voiced language that meaningfully resembles the AIOC+ V1 pedestrian-warning use case (Ace, Athens, Primo MSA language, a few construction pivots). The majority ask for video evidence + in-cab monitor. That is not an AI product.
2. The ADAS bucket with the most customer-voiced pull is "Moving-into-moving BSM lane change/turn" on Class-8 tractors — and that is exactly the segment where Birdseye today does not work (DAK confirmed). FirstFleet/Werner (2,600 veh + parent) is the named example. An AIOC+ tractor-capable BSM would be net-new coverage.
3. The "stationary blind-spot / curb ped" bucket is the one where Provision is being pitched successfully today (Ace, Athens, GreenWaste, Liberty) but is also where Ace flagged FPs. If AIOC+ V1 targets this bucket, the precision bar is already being set by a competitor we co-sell against — worth discussing against the "Accuracy is the brand" principle.
