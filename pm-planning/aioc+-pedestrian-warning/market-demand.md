## Matrix

### Row definitions
- **Backing / Reversing** — pedestrian or worker behind or beside vehicle during reverse maneuver (UC1, UC11, UC18, UC21, UC22, UC24)
- **Right-Turn Blind Spot** — nearside ped or cyclist during right turn at urban intersection or from curb (UC3, UC8, UC12, UC23)
- **Left-Turn Blind Spot** — offside ped crossing as vehicle turns left across oncoming traffic (no equivalent AIOC-V1 use case with explicit left-turn framing; UK DVS nearside = left only)

### Column definitions
- **Urban scenario** — dense pedestrian environment: intersections, bus stops, delivery routes, crosswalks, curbside stops in city grid
- **Highway / Yard scenario** — depot, landfill, loading dock, construction yard, residential pickup route (low-speed, off-road or residential)

## Recommendation

**Build backing/reversing first, anchored to the highway/yard scenario (waste residential pickup, depot, construction yard).** This cell has the highest pipeline signal by a wide margin: ~$11.9M open CCV and ~$7.4M closed-lost CCV, with the closed-lost dominated by "Motive Product Gap" — Republic Services ($3.6M), CEMEX ($2.87M), and the Staker & Parson verbatim ("they are ready to buy but we cannot do wireless"). These are customers who named Motive's product gap as the loss reason and in at least one case were standing at the door ready to sign. The fatality anchor is the strongest in the dataset: BLS puts refuse and recyclable material collectors as the 4th deadliest occupation in the US, with 69% of deaths as transportation incidents. NIOSH's backing-specific data (18% of vehicle-strike deaths during reversing) and OSHA's mandatory backup alarm standard mean this is a federally-quantified, regulated risk category — not a discretionary safety investment. The closed-won pattern confirms this vocation: 78% of all closed-won CCV in the 58-opp set came from waste and recycling accounts. Motive is already winning here; the gap is in the precision and wireless constraints of the current Omnicam + ProVision co-sell, not in customer intent.

**Right-turn blind spot in urban is the clear second priority, not a concurrent V1 target.** It has meaningful pipeline ($5.3M open, transit and school bus anchored), a hard regulatory tailwind (FTA Safety Advisory 23-1, NYC Vision Zero MTA deployment of 285 buses), and the largest single named verdict in the dataset ($38.8M Republic Services right-turn crosswalk death — which also signals waste sector litigation exposure on turning maneuvers). The problem is that Samsara's primary named use case for AI Multicam is "pedestrians near turning vehicles" — competing here directly means matching their headline feature at launch. The stronger entry is to win backing first (where Samsara's waste case studies predate AI Multicam and they have no named waste customer on Pedestrian Collision Warning), then expand to right-turn in V2 with a vocation-specific proof point. Left-turn has essentially no US pipeline signal and no standalone use-case ranking in the 32-UC framework — it is not a V1 or V2 consideration for the US market.
